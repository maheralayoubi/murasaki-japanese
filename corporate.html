Lessons to Regular Foreign Employees

Business Japanese skills are not equal to JLPT level

Although most companies require JLPT N1 or N2 of foreign applicants for employment, there are numerous cases where N1 holder cannot speak formal Japanese or communicate smoothly. MJI provides your foreign employees, who are in need of learning how to make the best use of Japanese language knowledge they have built, with practical Japanese lessons that unleash their talents.

  • Point 01

    Mock meeting textbook

    Using our original textbook, Mock Meeting, students can learn how to listen and speak in business conferences, and write conference summaries.

  • Point 02

    Task-focused curriculum

    Our lessons focus on achieving tasks such as writing business e-mails, talking with clients on the phone, doing a presentation, etc.

  • Point 03

    Counselling for students

    We provide each student with counselling in order to ease his/her stress and anxiety of living and working in a country with different language and culture.

As a result of our lessons, students will be able to :

  • do proper greetings in a variety of business settings
  • understand and practice business etiquette in Japan
  • speak politely in internal and external communication
  • speak on the phone
  • write business e-mails
  • behave professionally in business conferences
  • express their positive and negative opinions in formal way
  • give a logical explanation of business affairs
  • make a confirmation politely
  • give a presentation to introduce their new ideas

Other Corporate Services

  • Assistance for Job Interviews

    All the applicants for employment prepare answers to a variety of interview questions, so it is sometimes difficult to evaluate properly their language and communication skills. MJI can help your human resources team with arranging job interviews to foreign applicants, or conduct interviews on behalf. We have professional methods of revealing their real language and communication skills, which will be better information for your decision-making than stereotypical scripted answers.

  • Cross-cultural Seminar for Japanese Supervisors

    Foreign employees in Japanese companies are always required to understand Japanese corporate culture. Only with this one-sided cultutral understanding, however, they cannot build good relationship with their companies. Our cross-cultural seminar for Japanese supervisors introduce good tips on developing mutual undestanding between foreign employees and their companies. At a result of this seminar, perticipants will be able to communicate with foreign employees more smoothly, with respect to their cultural background.
