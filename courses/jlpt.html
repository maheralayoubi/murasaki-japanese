2-months intensive lessons for your success in JLPT

JLPT (Japanese Language Profociency Test) is an examination that certifies Japanese language proficiency of non-native Japanese speakers. If you think of getting a job in Japan, N2 level at least is required. Holding N1 will be greater advantage in job hunting and visa application. Starting 2 months before the date of the exam, MJI's JLPT Preparation Course offers valuable tips on how to figure out the answers of JLPT questions. We will be happy to help you aiming for the success in JLPT and your goals beyond.

Our Strengths

  • 2-months intensive lessons

    JLPT Preparation Course starts 2 months before the date of the exam. With our high quality lessons, full of effective practices and helpful tips, you can hugely improve your skills in a short period of time.

  • English explanations

    MJI instructors have been trained in how to explain vocabularies and grammar in English, so you will understand quickly and have more time to practice.

    Small group lessons will help you feel comfortable focus on your study more. You can also feel free to ask questions to your teacher because all students in the class are at the same Japanese level.

Curriculum

JLPT is composed of three elements: "Language Knowledge (Vocabulary/Grammar)", "Reading", and "Listening." You need to focus on refining not a particular skill you are confident with, but all of these skills, because both total score and score in each scoring section need to be above passing point. Our lessons are designd for improving thoroughly all skills you need for JLPT, without overlooking or neglecting your weekpoints.

Vocabulary & Grammar
  • Memorize high frequency words
  • Learn high frequency grammar
  • Practice figuring out the answers to Vocabulary and Grammar questions quickly
  • practice guessing the meaning of words and grammar you do not know
Reading
  • Learn tips for dealing with Reading questions
  • Practice reading quickly without overlooking necessary points
  • Practice reading between the lines
  • Practice guessing the meaning of words and grammar you do not know
Listening
  • Learn how to take memos
  • Learn how to catch Five Ws and One H
  • Practice shdowing
  • Practice guessing the meaning of sentences you did not catch clearly
