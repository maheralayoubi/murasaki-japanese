Our Strengths

  • Fun & Friendly Lessons

    In MJI's lessons for children, we use a variety of materials such as origami, puzzles, and blocks, so that your child can enjoy learning Japanese. Our friendly and caring teachers who love teaching Japanese to children will offer lessons full of fun and smiles.

  • Lesson plan by age

    We design lessons based on your child's growth stage. If your child is still small, we can learn simple Japanese words by playing picture cards and blocks together. If your child is a junior highschool student who is preparing for high school entrance exam, we can assist him/her with interview test practices.

  • Lessons at your home

    Our teachers will visit your home, your friend's house, or other convenient places in or around Tokyo for lessons. Your child can feel comfortable focus on learning without unnecessary stress.

Lesson Plan by Age

2 - 3 years old
  • Sing Japanese nursery rhymes and dancing
  • Learn Japanese vocabularies with picture-story show and picture books
  • Play picture cards and blocks
  • Learn shape names and color names by drawing
  • Speak in 2 word or 3 word sentence
4 - 5 years old
  • Sing Japanese nursery rhymes and dancing
  • Enrich vocabulary by playing origami, cat's cradle, and finger play
  • Play Simon Says Game in Japanese
  • learn hiragana and katakana with block puzzles
  • Learn simple Japanese expressions through playing house and store
6 - 10 years old
  • Read and write hiragana, katakana, numbers, and kanji of each grade
  • Greeting and Self-introduction in Japanese
  • Read short essays both silently and aloud and talk about them
  • Write essays of 400 - 800 characters
  • Learn Japanese expressions used in conversation with friends and teachers of school
10 - 12 years old
  • Small presentations
  • Read short essays both silently and aloud and discuss them
  • Write essays of 800 - 1200 characters
  • Japanese polite expressions
  • How to use public transportation
  • Conversational expressions used in school and stores
13 - 15 years old
  • Practice kanji in junior high school textbooks
  • Communicate properly in both casual and formal situations
  • Practice talking on the phone
  • Write essays in logical Japanese
  • Prepare for the interview test of high school entrance exam
